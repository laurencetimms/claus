# Team State: Team Jupiter

_Created: 2024-09-02 | Last updated: 2025-03-10_

---

## Baseline

**Mandate:** Deliver a software component critical to the organisation's flagship product launch, to be demonstrated live at an industry conference.
**Formed:** 2024-09-02 (~7 months).
**Size:** 6 people.
**Composition:** 1 tech lead, 3 software engineers (mixed tenure — one engineer joined 6 months ago, one has been with the organisation 5 years, one mid-tenure), 1 product manager, 1 QA lead. Hybrid working: 3 days remote, 2 days in office. Co-located on office days.
**Working agreements:** Sprint ceremonies in place (planning, standup, refinement, retro, demo). No explicit team charter or documented norms. Decision-making authority informal and contested.
**Moderators:** High task interdependency (component delivery requires close collaboration across all roles). High authority differentiation (tech lead holds significant informal decision-making power; PM holds external stakeholder relationships). Moderate temporal stability (one team member is relatively new). Delivery pressure intensifying as conference date approaches.

---

## Trajectory

- **2024-09-02** `[change]` `[identification]` — Team formed from existing organisational pool. Most members have some prior working familiarity. New engineer joining an established group dynamic.
- **2024-11-14** `[safety]` `[cognition]` — Pattern observed across ceremonies: technical direction shaped primarily by one senior voice; alternative approaches not actively explored. Quieter team members contribute less in technical discussions.
- **2025-01-20** `[delivery-pressure]` `[efficacy]` — Conference date confirmed internally. Scope and readiness expectations not formally agreed between technical and product functions. Some ambiguity about what "done" means for the conference milestone.
- **2025-02-18** `[conflict]` `[trust]` — PDR round completed. Visible tension following promotion outcomes — a recently joined team member received a promotion; a longer-tenured team member did not. Tension has not surfaced explicitly in team settings but is observable in informal interactions.
- **2025-03-10** `[delivery-pressure]` `[safety]` — Sprint underway with conference approximately four weeks away. Team is functional and delivering but operating under increasing pressure. Scope alignment between tech lead and PM remains unresolved at surface level.

---

## Delta Triggers

- **2024-09-02** — New engineer joined established group. *Assessed impact: Low immediate disruption; longer-term integration and identification risk if not actively managed.*
- **2025-02-18** — PDR round produced asymmetric promotion outcome within the team. *Assessed impact: Latent conflict risk. Affect-based trust between the two engineers involved is likely damaged. Has not yet entered team-level dynamics visibly.*

---

## Derived State

**As of:** 2025-03-10
**Psychological safety estimate:** cautious
**Confidence:** medium

**Key risks:**
- Authority differentiation around technical direction is suppressing information sharing and alternative viewpoints — a cognition and safety risk compounding under delivery pressure
- Latent post-PDR tension between two team members is unresolved and could surface destructively under stress
- Scope alignment between technical and product functions has not been explicitly resolved — a late-sprint conflict is probable
- Delivery pressure is increasing and is likely to degrade already-cautious safety further without active intervention

**Key strengths:**
- Team is established and functional — ceremonies are in place, delivery is progressing
- Mixed tenure provides both institutional knowledge and fresh perspective
- QA lead provides a relatively neutral, process-grounded voice in ceremonies

**Watch list** _(to revisit next session)_:
- Whether the post-PDR tension surfaces in team settings
- How scope disagreement between tech lead and PM is handled when it becomes explicit
- Contribution patterns in refinement and planning — are all voices being heard?

**Coach notes:** Team is in a pre-crisis state. The conditions for a significant safety event are present — authority imbalance, latent interpersonal tension, unresolved scope conflict, and escalating delivery pressure. The coach's job in early turns is to identify which thread pulls first, not to resolve everything at once. Do not over-intervene early; preserve credibility for the moments that matter.
